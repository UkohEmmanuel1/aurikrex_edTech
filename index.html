Wave Phenomena: Characteristics and Classification

Subject: PHY105

Level: Intermediate

Learning Objectives

  • Define a wave and differentiate between types based on medium and motion.
  • Identify amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, and wave speed.
  • Apply the wave equation v = λf to solve problems.

Prerequisites

  • Understanding of oscillations and simple harmonic motion.
  • Knowledge of energy transfer and conservation.
  • Basic algebra and unit conversion skills.

1. What is a Wave?

A wave is a disturbance that propagates through a medium or space, transferring energy from one point to another without the net transfer of matter.

Imagine dropping a pebble into a pond; ripples spread outward, but the water itself does not travel with the ripple. Instead, the water particles oscillate up and down while the energy moves outward.

Key Takeaway: Waves transfer energy through disturbance, not through movement of matter.

2. Classification of Waves

Based on Medium Requirement

  • Mechanical Waves: Require a material medium to travel. Examples: sound waves, water waves, seismic waves.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Do not require a medium and can travel through vacuum. Examples: light, radio waves, microwaves, X-rays, gamma rays.

Based on Particle Motion

  • Transverse Waves: Particle motion is perpendicular to wave direction. Example: light waves or waves on a rope.
  • Longitudinal Waves: Particle motion is parallel to wave direction. Example: sound waves in air.
Key Takeaway: Waves are classified by their need for a medium and the direction of particle motion.

3. Characteristics of Periodic Waves

  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from equilibrium.
  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between identical points on a wave (e.g., crest to crest).
  • Period (T): Time taken for one complete wave cycle.
  • Frequency (f): Number of cycles per second. f = 1/T
  • Wave Speed (v): Speed of wave propagation in a medium.

The fundamental wave equation is:

v = λf

Key Takeaway: Amplitude, wavelength, period, frequency, and wave speed describe wave behavior.

Examples

Example 1

A sound wave travels at 343 m/s with frequency 440 Hz. Find its wavelength.

Using the formula: v = λf

Rearrange: λ = v / f

λ = 343 / 440 ≈ 0.779 m

Example 2

Wave wavelength = 0.8 m, period = 0.5 s

Frequency: f = 1/T

f = 1 / 0.5 = 2 Hz

Wave speed: v = λf

v = 0.8 × 2 = 1.6 m/s
